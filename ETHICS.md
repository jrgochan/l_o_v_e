# Ethics

## Why This Exists

I built the L.O.V.E. Stack because I needed it. Not in the startup sense of "I identified a market gap." In the human sense of "I was trying to understand my own emotions and the tools didn't exist yet."

It helped me. Genuinely. The act of mapping feelings into geometry — giving them coordinates, watching them move through space — turned something overwhelming into something I could work with. Valence, Arousal, Connection. Three axes. A quaternion. Suddenly the chaos had shape.

I'm open-sourcing this because I believe emotion software should be a commons, not a commodity. If this helped me, it could help someone else. That matters more than a business model.

## What This Project Is For

- **Self-understanding.** Exploring your own emotional landscape with curiosity instead of judgment.
- **Therapeutic support.** Tools that therapists, counselors, and researchers can build on.
- **Education.** Teaching emotional intelligence through interactive, geometric visualization.
- **Research.** Advancing our understanding of affect, mood, and emotional dynamics.
- **Connection.** Helping people find language and shape for experiences that often feel wordless.

## What This Project Is NOT For

I want to be direct about this:

- **Not for surveillance.** Do not use this software to monitor people's emotions without their informed, ongoing consent.
- **Not for manipulation.** Do not use emotional data to exploit, persuade, or coerce. No dark patterns. No engagement-maximizing algorithms that prey on vulnerability.
- **Not for profiling.** Do not build systems that categorize, score, or judge people based on their emotional states — for hiring, policing, insurance, or any gatekeeping purpose.
- **Not for weaponization.** Do not use emotional data as a weapon — against individuals, communities, or populations.

The Apache 2.0 license gives you broad freedom to use this code. I'm asking you, human to human, to use that freedom well.

## On the Patent

The VAC (Valence-Arousal-Connection) model and quaternion-based emotional mapping system are the subject of U.S. Provisional Patent Application No. 63/962,600.

Here's the honest truth about why that exists: I filed it as protection. Not to build a moat. Not to sue people. Not to stop anyone from using these ideas. I filed it so that if a large company tried to patent these methods and lock them away, there would be documented prior art with my name on it.

The Apache 2.0 license includes an explicit patent grant. That means: **if you use this software, you have a license to practice the patented methods.** Full stop. The patent exists to protect the openness of this work, not to restrict it.

## On Making a Living

I'll be honest about this too. I'd love to make money from the years of work I've put into this. I have bills. I have a life. Building this wasn't free.

But I refuse to build a business on selling people's emotions. I thought about it. I tried to find a way. And every path that led to monetizing emotion data felt wrong in a way I couldn't talk myself out of.

So here's what I landed on: **this is free. It's open. It's yours to use.**

## A Note to Builders

If you fork this, extend it, build something new with it — I'd love to hear about it. Not because I need to approve it, but because knowing this helped someone else would genuinely make my day.

And if you're building something in the emotion space, I'd gently ask you to think about the ethics of what you're creating. Emotional data is some of the most intimate information a person can generate. Treat it with the care it deserves.

Build things that help people understand themselves better.
Don't build things that help corporations understand people better.
