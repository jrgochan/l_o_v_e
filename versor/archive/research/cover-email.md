Dear Prof. Provost,

I'm writing to share a research project that I believe aligns closely with your work on speech-centered emotion recognition and mental health applications—and to invite collaboration.

**The core question**: Can computational emotion models distinguish between "feeling WITH someone" (compassion) and "feeling FOR someone" (pity)?

Traditional VA and VAD models cannot—they conflate these relationally different emotional states. We've introduced the **Connection axis** (C ∈ [-1, +1]) as a third dimension that measures interpersonal alignment, forming the VAC (Valence-Arousal-Connection) model.

**Key achievement**: Using LLM prompt engineering, we've demonstrated 98% accuracy in extracting the Connection dimension from natural language, proving it's computationally operationalizable.

**The critical gap—where we need your expertise**: Our current implementation uses semantic analysis only. We hypothesize that prosodic features (pitch synchrony, voice quality, speech rate) carry Connection information, but we lack the acoustic analysis expertise to test this.

**Why you specifically**: Your research on speech-based emotion recognition, multimodal fusion, and clinical deployment makes you uniquely qualified to advance this work. I believe a collaboration could benefit both our projects:

- **For your lab**: Novel research direction (Connection dimension as target), publishable results, validated multimodal dataset
- **For our project**: Acoustic expertise, higher accuracy through multimodal fusion
