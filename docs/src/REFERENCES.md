## 1. Foundational Emotion Theory

The VAC model builds on decades of emotion research while introducing the novel Connection dimension.

### Traditional Dimensional Models

**James A. Russell (1980)** - Circumplex Model of Affect

- *Citation:* Russell, J. A. (1980). A Circumplex Model of Affect. *Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*, 39(6), 1161-1178. DOI: 10.1037/h0077714
- **Influence:** Established Valence-Arousal (VA) as the foundation for dimensional emotion models
- **Implementation:** VAC extends Russell's model by adding the Connection dimension

**Albert Mehrabian & James A. Russell (1974)** - PAD Model

- *Citation:* Mehrabian, A., & Russell, J. A. (1974). *An Approach to Environmental Psychology*. MIT Press.
- **Influence:** Introduced Dominance as a third dimension (Pleasure-Arousal-Dominance)
- **Implementation:** VAC replaces Dominance with Connection for relational quality rather than power dynamics

### Categorical Emotion Models

**Paul Ekman (1992)** - Basic Emotions Theory

- *Citation:* Ekman, P. (1992). An Argument for Basic Emotions. *Cognition and Emotion*, 6(3-4), 169-200. DOI: 10.1080/02699939208411068
- **Influence:** Identified universal emotional categories and facial expressions
- **Implementation:** Informed the categorical structure overlaid on VAC space

**Robert Plutchik (1980)** - Wheel of Emotions

- *Citation:* Plutchik, R. (1980). A General Psychoevolutionary Theory of Emotion. *Emotion: Theory, Research, and Experience*, 1, 3-33.
- **Influence:** Demonstrated how emotions can be organized spatially and combined
- **Implementation:** Inspired the 3D spatial organization of the 87-emotion atlas

**Alan S. Cowen & Dacher Keltner (2017)** - 27 Emotion Categories

- *Citation:* Cowen, A. S., & Keltner, D. (2017). Self-report captures 27 distinct categories of emotion bridged by continuous gradients. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 114(38), E7900-E7909. DOI: 10.1073/pnas.1702247114
- **Influence:** Demonstrated that rich categorical taxonomies can coexist with dimensional structure
- **Implementation:** Validated the approach of mapping 87 emotions onto continuous VAC space

**Lisa Feldman Barrett (2017)** - Theory of Constructed Emotion

- *Citation:* Barrett, L. F. (2017). *How Emotions Are Made: The Secret Life of the Brain*. Houghton Mifflin Harcourt.
- **Influence:** Highlighted the constructed and context-dependent nature of emotions
- **Implementation:** Informed semantic extraction approach using LLM contextual understanding

## 2. The VAC Model Innovation - Brené Brown's Work

The Connection axis is grounded in **Brené Brown's** 20+ years of research on vulnerability, shame, and human connection.

### Primary Source: Atlas of the Heart

**Brené Brown (2021)** - Atlas of the Heart

- *Citation:* Brown, B. (2021). *Atlas of the Heart: Mapping Meaningful Connection and the Language of Human Experience*. Random House. ISBN: 978-0399592553
- **Core Contribution:** Mapped 87 distinct emotions organized into 13 semantic categories
- **Implementation in L.O.V.E.:**
  - Complete 87-emotion atlas with VAC coordinates
  - 13 categorical groupings for pathfinding constraints
  - Relational distinctions (pity vs. compassion, shame vs. guilt)
  - Bridge emotion concepts (vulnerability, acceptance, self-compassion)

### Additional Brown Research

**Brené Brown (2012)** - Daring Greatly

- *Citation:* Brown, B. (2012). *Daring Greatly: How the Courage to Be Vulnerable Transforms the Way We Live, Love, Parent, and Lead*. Gotham Books.
- **Influence:** Established vulnerability as necessary for shame resilience
- **Implementation:** Vulnerability as required bridge emotion (Shame → Vulnerability → Self-Compassion)

**Brené Brown (2006)** - Shame Resilience Theory

- *Citation:* Brown, B. (2006). Shame Resilience Theory: A Grounded Theory Study on Women and Shame. *Families in Society*, 87(1), 43-52. DOI: 10.1606/1044-3894.3483
- **Influence:** Distinguished shame (identity-level) from guilt (behavior-level)
- **Implementation:** Shame has extreme negative Connection (-0.8), guilt has positive Connection (+0.4)

### Key Insight: Connection as a Dimension

Brown's work emphasizes the distinction between:

- **"Feeling FOR"** someone (pity, sympathy) → Negative Connection
- **"Feeling WITH"** someone (compassion, empathy) → Positive Connection

This relational quality became the **Connection axis**, the core innovation of the VAC model.

## 4. Mathematical Foundations

The Versor module transforms emotional vectors into smooth 3D rotations using quaternion mathematics.

### Quaternion Algebra

**William Rowan Hamilton (1843)** - Discovery of Quaternions

- *Citation:* Hamilton, W. R. (1843). On Quaternions; or on a New System of Imaginaries in Algebra. *Philosophical Magazine*, 25, 489-495.
- **Contribution:** Four-dimensional number system for representing rotations
- **Implementation:** VAC coordinates converted to quaternions [w, x, y, z] for 3D visualization
- **Why Quaternions:**
  - No gimbal lock (unlike Euler angles)
  - Smooth interpolation (SLERP)
  - Computationally efficient
  - Unique shortest-path rotation

### Spherical Linear Interpolation (SLERP)

**Ken Shoemake (1985)** - SLERP Algorithm

- *Citation:* Shoemake, K. (1985). Animating Rotation with Quaternion Curves. *ACM SIGGRAPH Computer Graphics*, 19(3), 245-254. DOI: 10.1145/325165.325242
- **Contribution:** Constant angular velocity interpolation between quaternions
- **Implementation:**
  - 60-frame animation paths between emotional states
  - Smooth "Soul Sphere" rotations in Experience module
  - Maintains constant angular velocity throughout transition

## 5. Computer Science & Algorithms

### A* Pathfinding Algorithm

**Peter E. Hart, Nils J. Nilsson, & Bertram Raphael (1968)** - A* Algorithm

- *Citation:* Hart, P. E., Nilsson, N. J., & Raphael, B. (1968). A Formal Basis for the Heuristic Determination of Minimum Cost Paths. *IEEE Transactions on Systems Science and Cybernetics*, 4(2), 100-107. DOI: 10.1109/TSSC.1968.300136
- **Contribution:** Optimal graph search using heuristic estimates
- **Implementation in Observer:**
  - Therapeutic pathfinding between emotional states
  - Respects psychological constraints (category boundaries)
  - Heuristic: straight-line VAC distance (admissible)
  - Prevents "toxic positivity" (inappropriate shortcuts like Grief → Joy)
  - Requires bridge emotions (Shame → Vulnerability → Self-Compassion)

### Vector Similarity Search

**HNSW Algorithm** - Hierarchical Navigable Small World

- **Contribution:** Fast approximate nearest neighbor search in high-dimensional spaces
- **Implementation:**
  - pgvector extension with HNSW indexing
  - Finding similar past emotional moments (semantic search)
  - Sub-50ms queries even with 1M+ trajectory points

### Vector Database Technology

**Andrew Johnson (2023)** - pgvector

- *Citation:* Johnson, A. (2023). pgvector: Open-source vector similarity search for Postgres. GitHub. <https://github.com/pgvector/pgvector>
- **Contribution:** PostgreSQL extension for vector operations
- **Implementation:**
  - 384-dimensional semantic embeddings
  - HNSW indexing for fast similarity search
  - Integration with PostgreSQL ACID properties
