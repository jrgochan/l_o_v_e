# Glossary - Versor Module

Comprehensive terminology reference for the Versor module.

---

## A

**Angular Distance (φ)**
The angle of rotation required to transform one quaternion to another, measured in radians or degrees. Formula: `φ = 2 × arccos(|w_transition|)`. Represents the "emotional work" needed to shift between states.

**Arousal**
The second axis of the VAC model, representing energy level from calm (-1.0) to excited (+1.0). Physiologically related to activation of the sympathetic nervous system.

**Axis-Angle Representation**
A way to represent rotations using a unit axis vector and a rotation angle around that axis. Quaternions can be constructed from axis-angle: `q = [cos(θ/2), sin(θ/2)·axis]`.

---

## C

**Connection**
The third axis of the VAC model (our innovation), representing relational alignment from disconnected (-1.0) to connected (+1.0). Differentiates emotions like pity (separated) from compassion (connected).

**Conjugate**
The quaternion conjugate flips the sign of the vector part: `q̅ = [w, -x, -y, -z]`. For unit quaternions, the conjugate equals the inverse. Represents the reverse rotation.

**CORS (Cross-Origin Resource Sharing)**
HTTP headers that allow the Versor API to be called from web browsers running on different origins (e.g., Experience module).

---

## D

**Double-Cover Property**
The mathematical property where quaternions `q` and `-q` represent the same rotation. This is why SLERP must check dot products to ensure the shortest path.

**Dominant Axis**
The VAC dimension (Valence, Arousal, or Connection) that changed the most in a transition. Used to generate insights like "AROUSAL_SHIFT".

---

## E

**Elasticity (E)**
The rate of emotional change, calculated as angular distance divided by time: `E = φ / Δt` (radians per second). High elasticity (> 2.0 rad/s) indicates rapid emotional shifts (flooding).

**Epsilon (ε)**
A small number (typically 1e-6) used as a tolerance for floating-point comparisons. Used to handle numerical precision issues.

**Euler Angles**
A rotation representation using three angles (pitch, yaw, roll). Suffers from gimbal lock. Rejected in favor of quaternions for the Versor.

---

## F

**Flooding**
Emotional overwhelm detected when elasticity exceeds a threshold (default: 2.0 rad/s). Indicates rapid emotional changes that may require therapeutic intervention.

---

## G

**Geodesic Distance**
The shortest path between two points on a curved surface. For quaternions, the geodesic distance on the 4D unit sphere measures rotational similarity.

**Gimbal Lock**
A mathematical singularity in Euler angle representation where two rotation axes align, losing a degree of freedom. Cannot occur with quaternions.

---

## H

**Hamilton Product**
The special multiplication operation for quaternions. Non-commutative: `q₁ * q₂ ≠ q₂ * q₁`.

**HNSW (Hierarchical Navigable Small World)**
An algorithm used by Observer for vector similarity search. Not used in Versor (which is stateless).

---

## I

**Identity Quaternion**
The quaternion representing no rotation: `[1, 0, 0, 0]`. Returned for zero VAC vectors.

**Interpolation Path**
A sequence of intermediate quaternions between start and end states, generated by SLERP. Used by Experience module for smooth animation.

---

## L

**LERP (Linear Interpolation)**
Simple linear interpolation: `(1-t)·a + t·b`. Not suitable for quaternions because it doesn't stay on the unit sphere.

---

## M

**Magnitude**
The length of a vector or quaternion. For VAC: `||v|| = √(v² + a² + c²)`. For quaternion: `||q|| = √(w² + x² + y² + z²)`. Unit quaternions have magnitude 1.0.

---

## N

**NLERP (Normalized Linear Interpolation)**
Linear interpolation followed by normalization. Faster than SLERP but has variable angular velocity. Not used in Versor (SLERP preferred).

**Normalization**
Dividing a vector or quaternion by its magnitude to get unit length. Critical for quaternions to represent pure rotations.

---

## Q

**Quaternion**
A 4D number `[w, x, y, z]` representing rotations in 3D space. Avoids gimbal lock and enables smooth interpolation (SLERP).

---

## S

**Scalar-First Convention**
Quaternion notation with scalar component first: `[w, x, y, z]`. Used by L.O.V.E. (matches mathematical literature). Contrast with scalar-last `[x, y, z, w]` used by SciPy/robotics.

**SLERP (Spherical Linear Interpolation)**
The algorithm for smoothly interpolating between quaternions while maintaining constant angular velocity and staying on the unit sphere. Invented by Ken Shoemake (1985).

**Soul Sphere**
The 3D visualization in the Experience module showing the user's emotional state as a rotating sphere. Versor provides the quaternions for rotation.

**Stateless**
Architectural property where each request is independent with no stored state. Enables trivial horizontal scaling. Versor stores nothing between requests.

---

## T

**Transition Quaternion**
The quaternion representing the rotation from one emotional state to another. Formula: `q_transition = q_start^(-1) * q_target`.

---

## U

**Unit Quaternion**
A quaternion with magnitude 1.0. Only unit quaternions represent pure rotations (no scaling). All quaternions in Versor are unit quaternions.

**Unit Sphere (S³)**
The 4D sphere of radius 1. All unit quaternions lie on S³. SLERP follows great circle arcs on this sphere.

---

## V

**VAC (Valence-Arousal-Connection)**
The 3D emotional model used by L.O.V.E.:

- Valence: Pleasant (+) to unpleasant (-)
- Arousal: Energized (+) to calm (-)
- Connection: Connected (+) to disconnected (-)

Extends Russell's VAD model by replacing Dominance with Connection.

**VAD (Valence-Arousal-Dominance)**
Traditional 3D emotional model. Replaced by VAC in L.O.V.E. because Connection is more therapeutically relevant than Dominance.

**Valence**
The first axis of the VAC model, representing pleasantness from negative (-1.0) to positive (+1.0). Corresponds to the X-axis in quaternion representation.

**Versor**

1. The Versor module (this documentation).
2. In mathematics: A unit quaternion.
3. Etymology: Latin "versor" = "turner" (that which turns/rotates).

## Key Concepts

### Gimbal Lock

A mathematical singularity in Euler angle rotation representation where two rotation axes align, losing a degree of freedom. Makes certain rotations impossible to represent. Does not occur with quaternions.

**Emotional metaphor:** Being emotionally "stuck" with no perspective to pivot.

### Pity→Compassion Test

The critical semantic test that validates the Connection axis works correctly. Pity and compassion have identical valence and arousal but opposite connection, proving the VAC model can differentiate them.

### Scalar-First vs Scalar-Last

Two different quaternion notations:

- **Scalar-first:** `[w, x, y, z]` - Used by L.O.V.E., game engines, physics
- **Scalar-last:** `[x, y, z, w]` - Used by SciPy, robotics (ROS)

Requires conversion when calling SciPy functions.
