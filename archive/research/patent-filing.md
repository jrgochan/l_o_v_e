## Filing Methods

### Option A: Electronic Filing via EFS-Web (Recommended)

**Advantages:**
- Lower fees ($25 discount)
- Instant confirmation
- Track application status online
- Required for most patent offices now

**Steps:**

1. **Create USPTO Account**
   - Visit: https://my.uspto.gov/
   - Register for an account
   - Obtain Customer Number (optional but helpful)

2. **Prepare Documents**
   - Convert all .md files to PDF
   - Or: Use pandoc to convert: `pandoc 00-COVER-PAGE.md -o cover-page.pdf`
   - Combine or keep separate (USPTO accepts either)

3. **Access EFS-Web**
   - Visit: https://efs.uspto.gov/
   - Log in with your USPTO account
   - Select "File a New Application"
   - Choose "Provisional Application"

4. **Upload Documents**
   - Application Data Sheet (optional for provisional)
   - Specification: All sections combined or separate PDFs
   - Claims: Include even though not required for provisional
   - Drawings: If you create diagrams (currently not included)

5. **Pay Fees**
   - Micro entity: $75 + $45 (excess claims) = $120
   - Small entity: $150 + $90 = $240
   - Large entity: $300 + $180 = $480
   - Payment via credit card or deposit account

6. **Submit & Confirm**
   - Review all information
   - Certify accuracy
   - Submit application
   - Save confirmation number immediately
   - Download receipt PDF

### Option B: Mail Filing (Not Recommended)

**Only use if electronic filing is impossible.**

**Mail to:**

   - Identify potential partners/customers

### Month 10-11: Prepare Utility Patent Application

**You MUST convert to utility patent within 12 months or lose priority date.**

**Utility Patent Requirements:**
- Formal claims (your provisional claims are a good start)
- Detailed description with working examples
- Professional drawings (hire patent illustrator ~$500-$1,500)
- Prior art search ($500-$2,000 if professional)
- Attorney preparation ($5,000-$15,000)

**Total utility patent cost: $8,000-$20,000**

### Month 12: File Utility Patent (Deadline!)

**Options:**

**Option 1: Convert to Full US Utility Patent**
- Claims priority from provisional
- Examination process begins (2-3 years typically)
- Cost: ~$10,000-$20,000 total

**Option 2: File PCT (International)**
- Patent Cooperation Treaty application
- Preserves rights in 150+ countries
- Gives 18 more months to decide which countries
- Cost: ~$4,000-$8,000 filing + per-country costs later

**Option 3: Abandon (if strategic)**
- No further costs
- Invention becomes prior art (blocks others)
- Provisional never published

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Missing the 12-month deadline** - You lose everything
❌ **Public disclosure before filing** - Destroys patentability in most countries
❌ **Inadequate description** - Can't add new matter later
❌ **Not consulting attorney** - Expensive mistakes in claims
❌ **Ignoring Dr. Brown's contribution** - Ethical and legal liability
❌ **Filing in wrong entity status** - Pay wrong fees, complications later

✅ **Do This Instead:**
- Set calendar reminders for month 6, 9, 10, 11
- Document everything (lab notebooks, timestamps)
- Consult attorney before utility patent filing
- Address Dr. Brown collaboration early
- Keep records of all development work
